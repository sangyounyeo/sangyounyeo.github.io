Foundations Reflection

Reflect on your experience of Core Learning during Foundations

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I have learnt that I am more capable than I have previously thought, my ego affects me more than I would like and the importance of self reflection. Through research of neuroplasticity and growth mindset I have learnt that I am in control of my learning and that I can change to better myself as a programmer and as a student of life. I have also learnt that my ego has been a major factor in hold me back by prevent me from having a growth mindset. Both of the first two can be assisted by self reflection as it helps me come up with better plans and learn better by figuring out what I did wrong and well and also by keeping my ego in check.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

The roles of values is that it guides and influence my decisions allowing me to make ethical choices, set goals that are meaningful to me therefor more likely to accomplish.
Empathy allows me to undestand the needs of others, whether it be clients, coworkers or teachers and helps me make effective communication, teamwork and solve problems.
Self-awareness helps me keep in check and allows me to realize my strengths, weaknessess make make effective plans and also allow constant improvement. I will be able to monitor my progress and adapt to my needs to imporove my learning.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

The amount of help that it has brought me. I have tried many things to make myself more productive but the core learning has taken me a step further in becoming a better learner and student.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I believe it is because it will allow students to cope with the intense workload (especially the full-time students) by giving them the necessary 'core' skills to adapt to this course and further along the line, in their careers.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No it has definitely not been a waste of time. I believe I was able to learn as much programming I have to this point due to the core learning acting as a support system giving me the knowledge and self belief to accomplish what I have to this date.
